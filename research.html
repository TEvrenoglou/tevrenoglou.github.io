Research activities

Scientific Interests

My research focuses on advancing statistical methodologies in meta-analysis, particularly addressing challenges with sparse data in network meta-analysis. I develop and validate novel statistical approaches to improve evidence synthesis in medical research.

Research Areas

  • Network Meta-Analysis
  • Pairwise Meta-Analysis
  • Sparse Data
  • Evidence Synthesis
  • Multiple Treatment Hierarchies

Current Projects

I am currently the principal investigator of a DFG-funded project titled "Multivariate methods on evidence synthesis: extending their framework and applicability". The project aims to enable the use of multivariate network meta-analysis in clinical applications and decision-making by developing novel statistical methods that provide treatment rankings across multiple treatments and outcomes. In addition, novel statistical R software will be developed as part of this project.
